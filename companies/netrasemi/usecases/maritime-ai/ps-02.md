# PS-02: Illegal Fishing Detection and Marine Reserve Monitoring

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Maritime AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Solar-Powered Buoy + Camera + Acoustic Sensor

---

## Problem Statement

Illegal, Unreported, and Unregulated (IUU) fishing costs the global economy $23B annually and depletes fish stocks that hundreds of millions of people depend on for food security and livelihoods. In India, marine protected areas and fishing exclusion zones are routinely violated because monitoring relies on patrol vessels — expensive, slow, and unable to cover India's 7,500km coastline. Legal artisanal fishermen suffer because IUU fishing by large commercial trawlers destroys the fish stocks they depend on.

Current satellite-based monitoring systems identify suspicious vessel behavior with hours to days of latency — useless for real-time enforcement.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Coastal artisanal fishing communities (300,000+ fishing families in India)
- State fisheries departments responsible for stock management
- Coast Guard and Fisheries Survey of India
- Marine protected area management authorities
- Consumers and seafood exporters dependent on sustainable supply chains

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India's fishing sector employs 14+ million people directly
- Fish stocks in key zones (Gulf of Mannar, Lakshadweep, Sundarbans) are under critical threat
- IUU-linked seafood is blocked from EU and US markets — a direct export impact
- Coral reef and mangrove ecosystem damage from illegal trawling is irreversible

---

## Proposed Solution

Solar-powered autonomous edge AI buoys — Netrasemi chip + underwater acoustic hydrophone + surface camera — deployed across marine protected area boundaries and fishing exclusion zones. Each buoy monitors for: (1) vessel presence via acoustic signature (trawler engines have distinctive signatures), (2) illegal trawling activity via underwater vibration pattern, and (3) vessel identity via surface camera + AIS cross-reference. Alert events are transmitted via satellite or LoRa to a Coast Guard or fisheries department control room.

**Key features:**
- 30-day autonomous deployment on solar + battery
- Underwater acoustic engine signature classification
- Surface vessel detection and direction tracking
- Low-bandwidth alert transmission (satellite SMS equivalent)
- Tamper-resistant enclosure; GPS tracking for buoy drift
- Scalable: deploy a network of 50–500 buoys across a marine protected area

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Marine buoys have no connectivity except low-bandwidth satellite (expensive per byte)
- Continuous acoustic and video data cannot be transmitted — only processed events
- Real-time detection (vessel entering zone) must happen at the buoy, not in the cloud
- Solar power budget is severely limited — only low-power edge compute is viable

## Feasibility

Acoustic vessel classification is a validated technique. Surface vessel detection from a buoy-mounted camera is feasible though challenging (wave motion, horizon, sea glare). The power budget is tight but manageable with Netrasemi's low-power architecture. Key challenge: buoy mechanical design for long-term survival in open sea conditions.

**Feasibility Score: 6/10**

## Possible MVP

A land-based prototype demonstrating acoustic vessel classification accuracy (trawler vs. artisanal fishing boat vs. cargo vessel) deployed as a fixed shore-side sensor at 3 coastal monitoring points, over a 3-month pilot.

**Timeline:** 12 weeks prototype, 3 months coastal pilot

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Buoy damage/loss in rough sea conditions | High | Ruggedized design; GPS recovery; insurance |
| Vandalism by illegal fishing operators | Medium | Stealth deployment; decoy buoys |
| Low-bandwidth satellite cost | High | Process-on-device; transmit only alert events (< 500 bytes each) |
| False alarms from whale/dolphin acoustic signatures | Medium | Multi-class acoustic model including marine mammals |
