# Evaluation Framework

> A structured lens for assessing innovation opportunities in Beyond Borders.

---

## Overview

Not every problem is worth solving. Not every technology creates a business. Not every market timing is right.

The Beyond Borders Evaluation Framework helps contributors, founders, and mentors rigorously assess whether a problem statement, use case, or startup opportunity is worth serious investment of time and resources.

The framework evaluates four dimensions: **Desirability**, **Feasibility**, **Viability**, and **Strategic Fit**.

---

## Dimension 1: Desirability

*Does someone urgently want this?*

Desirability measures the strength and urgency of demand for the solution.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Critical pain; users would pay immediately if a solution existed |
| 7–8 | Strong pain; significant demand once solution is demonstrated |
| 5–6 | Real pain, but with workarounds; demand requires convincing |
| 3–4 | Nice to have; not currently causing active pain |
| 1–2 | Solution in search of a problem |

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Who faces this problem, and how many of them are there?
- How frequently do they experience the pain?
- What are they currently doing to solve it (workaround cost)?
- Have you spoken to 5+ people who face this problem?
- Would they pay for a solution today if it existed?

---

## Dimension 2: Feasibility

*Can this actually be built with current technology?*

Feasibility measures whether the proposed solution can be built with technology available today or in the near term.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Core technology exists; primarily integration and UX work |
| 7–8 | Technology is research-proven; engineering is significant but clear |
| 5–6 | Key components exist but integration is unproven at required scale |
| 3–4 | Requires significant technology breakthroughs |
| 1–2 | Science fiction level — not buildable with current knowledge |

**Evaluation Questions:**
- What is the most technically uncertain component?
- Has this approach been demonstrated in any research or prototype?
- What is the minimum viable technical capability for the MVP?
- What skills are required in the founding team?
- How long will engineering take to first working prototype?

---

## Dimension 3: Viability

*Can this become a sustainable business?*

Viability measures the strength of the business model and path to revenue.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Clear buyer, clear price, repeat revenue, strong unit economics |
| 7–8 | Revenue model identified; some uncertainty on pricing or scale |
| 5–6 | Value creation is clear but monetization path requires validation |
| 3–4 | Unclear who pays or how much; significant business model risk |
| 1–2 | No clear revenue path |

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Who pays — the user, an intermediary, or the government?
- What is the realistic price point and purchasing process?
- What are the unit economics at scale?
- What is the customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value?
- Is there a clear path to first ₹1 crore ($120K) in revenue?

---

## Dimension 4: Strategic Fit

*Why this team, this technology, this moment?*

Strategic fit evaluates the timing, team, and technology advantage.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Unique technology advantage + perfect timing + right team |
| 7–8 | Strong advantage in at least 2 of 3 dimensions |
| 5–6 | Advantage in 1 dimension; catching up on others |
| 3–4 | Similar to existing solutions; no clear differentiation |
| 1–2 | Late to market with no differentiation |

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Why is now the right time? (What changed recently?)
- What does this team know/have that others don't?
- Why is this technology (Edge AI) the right approach vs. alternatives?
- What does the competitive landscape look like in 2 years?

---

## Scoring and Thresholds

| Overall Score | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| 8.0–10 | High priority — pursue aggressively |
| 6.5–7.9 | Strong candidate — validate key uncertainties first |
| 5.0–6.4 | Interesting but risky — address specific weak dimensions |
| Below 5.0 | Not recommended without major rethinking |

**Note:** A score of 10/10 on Desirability does not compensate for 3/10 on Viability. All four dimensions must reach at least 6/10 for a problem to be worth pursuing as a startup.

---

## How to Use This Framework

1. Score each dimension independently before discussing with others (avoid anchoring bias)
2. Document the evidence behind each score — not just the number
3. Identify the single biggest uncertainty in your assessment
4. Use the score as a starting point for conversation, not a final verdict
5. Revisit scores after talking to 5 potential customers
