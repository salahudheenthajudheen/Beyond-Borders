# PS-01: Vessel Collision Avoidance and Port Traffic Intelligence

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Maritime AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Multi-Camera + Radar Fusion

---

## Problem Statement

India has 12 major ports and 200+ minor ports handling 1.4 billion tonnes of cargo annually. Vessel navigation in and around port approaches is one of the highest-risk maritime environments — shallow water, congested traffic, narrow channels, and poor visibility during monsoon, fog, and night operations. Maritime collisions in Indian ports cause significant cargo damage, vessel loss, oil spills, and crew fatalities. The existing Vessel Traffic Service (VTS) infrastructure is operator-dependent, radar-only, and provides no AI-assisted collision prediction.

For smaller vessels — fishing boats, coastal cargo ferries, inland waterway vessels — there is no collision avoidance intelligence at all.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Port VTS (Vessel Traffic Service) operators managing ship movements
- Vessel captains and officers navigating port approaches
- Fishing vessel operators in heavily trafficked coastal zones
- Port authorities responsible for safety and insurance
- Coast Guard units monitoring unauthorized vessel movement

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- A single vessel collision in a major port causes $50M–$500M in cargo loss and vessel damage
- Oil spills from maritime accidents cause irreversible environmental damage to coastal ecosystems
- India's Sagarmala program is investing $9B in port modernization — safety intelligence is a core requirement
- 5,000+ Indian fishermen are lost at sea annually, partly from vessel collisions in fishing zones

---

## Proposed Solution

An edge AI maritime safety unit — Netrasemi chip + high-resolution cameras (RGB and thermal) + AIS (Automatic Identification System) receiver — deployed on vessel bridges and at port approach towers. The system fuses camera vision and AIS data to track all vessels in the vicinity, predict potential collision courses in real time, and alert navigators with specific recommended evasive maneuvers. Onshore units feed into a port-level traffic intelligence display for VTS operators.

**Key features:**
- Real-time vessel detection and tracking (camera + AIS fusion)
- CPA (Closest Point of Approach) and TCPA (Time to CPA) calculation for all detected vessels
- Collision risk alert: audible + visual on bridge display, < 2 second latency
- Night and adverse weather capability via thermal imaging
- No internet dependency: operates fully standalone on vessel
- Logs all vessel interactions for incident investigation

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Maritime environments have no internet connectivity at sea or in port approaches
- Latency of cloud processing is unacceptable for real-time collision avoidance
- Camera video from multiple angles is too large to transmit in real time
- Vessel navigation data is sensitive operational intelligence

## Feasibility

Vessel detection from camera + AIS fusion is well-studied. Object tracking in maritime environments has specific datasets (Singapore Maritime Dataset, ABOships). CPA/TCPA calculation is classical navigation math. The engineering challenge is robust performance in rain, fog, and sea glare conditions.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**
