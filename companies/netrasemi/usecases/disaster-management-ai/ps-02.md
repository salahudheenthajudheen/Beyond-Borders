# PS-02: Structural Collapse Detection and Survivor Location in Post-Earthquake Response

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Disaster Management AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Acoustic Sensors + Thermal Camera + Drone Integration

---

## Problem Statement

After a major earthquake, the survival probability of trapped survivors drops from 90% in the first hour to below 30% after 72 hours. The primary bottleneck in rescue operations is locating survivors trapped under rubble — a process that currently depends on human hearing, trained search dogs, and expensive acoustic search equipment that few SDRF teams possess. India's NDRF teams have 16 battalions for a country of 1.4 billion, covering earthquake-prone zones from Gujarat to Assam to Uttarakhand.

The 2023 Turkey earthquake killed 50,000+ people; experts estimate 30–40% of those deaths could have been prevented with better survivor location technology.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- NDRF and SDRF rescue teams
- Municipal search and rescue units
- International disaster response teams (UN INSARAG)
- Building owners and structural engineers in earthquake zones
- Families of trapped survivors

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India has a BIS Zone V and Zone IV earthquake belt covering 59% of its land area
- Rapid urbanization has created massive stocks of non-earthquake-resistant buildings in high-risk zones
- Golden hour in structural collapse rescue is 6 hours — current search methods miss most of it
- Climate change is amplifying secondary disasters (landslides after earthquakes) that create additional collapse events

---

## Proposed Solution

A portable edge AI search unit — Netrasemi chip + contact acoustic sensor array + thermal camera + seismic detector — that NDRF teams deploy at a collapse site. The device is placed on or near rubble; its acoustic sensors listen for human voice signatures, tapping, and breath sounds while the thermal camera searches for heat signatures through rubble gaps. The Netrasemi chip runs a real-time survivor detection model on the fused sensor data, providing rescuers with a directional vector and estimated depth to the survivor.

**Key features:**
- Detects voice, tapping, and breathing through 2–3 meters of concrete rubble
- Thermal signature detection through rubble voids and air gaps
- Noise-canceling AI: filters out equipment noise, secondary collapse sounds, and other rescuers
- Triangulates survivor location from multiple sensor placement points
- GPS + vector output: shows rescue teams exactly where to dig
- Fully battery-powered; deployable within 60 seconds of arrival
- Works in complete darkness, dust, and partial gas atmosphere

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Disaster sites have no infrastructure — no power, no internet
- Multi-sensor fusion (acoustic + thermal + seismic) requires real-time on-device computation
- Alert latency is a direct life-safety variable — every second of processing delay translates to excavation time lost
- Battery-powered portable device cannot sustain cloud transmission of raw sensor streams

## Feasibility

Acoustic survivor detection technology exists (Delsar, LifeLocator) but costs $50,000–$150,000 per unit and lacks AI analysis. Thermal survivor detection through rubble is an active research area. Edge AI fusion of both signals is technically feasible on Netrasemi chips. Key challenge: building a sufficient training dataset from actual collapse scenarios and controlled simulation environments.

**Feasibility Score: 6/10**

## Data Required

| Background noise profiles from real collapse sites | NDRF exercise recordings | Low |
| Real rescue outcome data | NDRF incident reports | High |

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Building training dataset from rare real events | High | Controlled simulation at NDRF training centers; synthetic data |
| False negatives (missed survivors) causing legal liability | High | Position as decision-support tool; never replace human hearing entirely |
| Device durability in extreme collapse environments | High | Military-grade ruggedization; IP68 + drop/crush resistance |
| Acoustic interference in multi-team rescue operations | High | Team coordination protocol; frequency isolation |
