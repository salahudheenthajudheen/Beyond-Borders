# PS-01: Edge AI Early Warning System for Flash Floods in River-Valley Communities

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Disaster Management AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Multi-Sensor River Monitoring Node

---

## Problem Statement

Flash floods kill 2,000+ people in India annually and displace millions. They strike with 15–60 minutes of warning — too short for communities to evacuate using current early warning systems that rely on manual rain gauge readings, human observation, and centralized meteorological analysis. In river-valley communities — the Western Ghats, Himalayan foothills, Northeast India — the terrain is too complex for satellite-based flood prediction to provide local, precise, actionable warnings.

India's existing FFEWS (Flash Flood Early Warning System) infrastructure covers only major rivers. The 50,000+ smaller rivers and streams that generate most flash flood casualties are unmonitored.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Rural communities living on river banks and in flood-prone valleys
- District administration and SDRF (State Disaster Response Force) teams
- Village panchayats responsible for community alert systems
- NDMA and state disaster management authorities
- Infrastructure operators (roads, bridges, power stations in flood-prone zones)

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- 80% of India's flood casualties occur from flash floods in under-monitored river systems
- A 30-minute evacuation warning reduces casualties by 60–80%
- Economic losses from flash floods are $3–5B annually in India, mostly in infrastructure damage
- Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of flash flood events

---

## Proposed Solution

A solar-powered, edge AI river monitoring node — Netrasemi chip + ultrasonic water level sensor + rain gauge + camera — deployed at 3–5 strategic points along a river reach. Each node runs an ML model that fuses water level rate-of-change, upstream rainfall intensity, and visual assessment of river turbidity and debris loading to predict flash flood risk 30–90 minutes ahead. Alert events (pre-defined thresholds) trigger immediate SMS alerts to village panchayat leaders, SDRF officers, and community siren systems — all without internet dependency.

**Key features:**
- Predicts flood risk 30–90 min ahead (vs. 5–10 min for reactive threshold alerts)
- Fuses multiple sensor types for higher accuracy than single rain gauge systems
- Operates fully offline; transmits via SMS gateway (2G coverage sufficient)
- Self-powered for 60+ days via solar
- Community alert integration: automated village siren trigger via GSM relay
- Dashboard for district administration and NDMA when connectivity available

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- River monitoring sites are remote; no internet connectivity
- Multi-sensor fusion for real-time flood prediction must happen at the node
- SMS transmission bandwidth cannot carry raw sensor streams — only processed predictions
- Battery/solar power budget is severe — cloud-dependent processing is not viable

---

## Desirability

Communities want warnings that give them enough time to evacuate. District administrators want credible, actionable early warning they can act on. NDMA wants technology that can scale to thousands of unmonitored river reaches at low cost. State governments want to reduce political fallout from preventable flood casualties.

**Desirability Score: 10/10**

---

## Feasibility

Water level sensors and rain gauges are mature, cheap technology. River flood prediction ML models are an active research area. Edge deployment of time-series prediction models (LSTM, random forest) on Netrasemi chips is feasible. The primary challenge is calibrating site-specific prediction models — every river reach has different hydrological characteristics.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

---

## Viability

**Revenue models:**

## Possible MVP

A 3-node monitoring system on one small river reach in a flood-prone district, running during one monsoon season (June–September). Track: prediction accuracy, warning lead time, community awareness and response rate.

**Timeline:** 10 weeks hardware prototype, one monsoon season for validation

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Sensor damage/loss during flood events | High | Elevated mounting; flood-hardened enclosure; post-flood replacement protocol |
| False alarms causing community alert fatigue | High | Calibrate model for high specificity; tiered alert levels |
| Solar panel damage in monsoon conditions | Medium | Ruggedized panel mounting; battery backup for 14 cloudy days |
| Political issues with failed warning events | High | Clear communication of system limitations; position as decision support, not guarantee |
