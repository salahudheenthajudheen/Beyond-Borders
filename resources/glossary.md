# Glossary

> Key terms used across the Beyond Borders repository.

---

## A

**Actuator**
A device that converts an AI decision into a physical action — opening a valve, triggering an alarm, moving a robot arm.

**AIS (Automatic Identification System)**
A maritime tracking system that transmits vessel identity, position, speed, and course. Used for collision avoidance and vessel traffic management.

**Anomaly Detection**
An AI technique that identifies patterns that deviate significantly from a learned baseline — used for predictive maintenance, fraud detection, and safety monitoring.

**AutoML**
Automated Machine Learning — tools that automate the process of building, training, and optimizing ML models, reducing the need for specialized ML expertise.

---

## B

**Batch Processing**
Processing data in large groups at scheduled intervals (e.g., overnight). Contrast with real-time inference.

**Biofouling**
The accumulation of marine organisms (barnacles, algae, mussels) on ship hulls and underwater structures, increasing drag and fuel consumption.

---

## C

**CDSCO**
Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation — India's regulatory body for medical devices and pharmaceutical products. AI diagnostic tools require CDSCO approval as Software as a Medical Device (SaMD).

**CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)**
A class of deep learning models particularly effective for image and video analysis. The backbone of most computer vision applications.

**Computer Vision**
A field of AI that enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world — images, video, depth data.

**CPA (Closest Point of Approach)**
A maritime navigation calculation that determines how close two vessels will come to each other if they maintain their current course and speed.

**CWSI (Crop Water Stress Index)**
A measure of plant water stress calculated from canopy temperature vs. ambient temperature, used to determine optimal irrigation timing.

---

## E

**Edge AI**
AI inference that runs on the device where data is generated, rather than in a centralized cloud server. Enables real-time, offline, privacy-preserving intelligence.

**Edge Inference**
The process of running a trained machine learning model on an edge device (vs. in the cloud).

**Explainability (XAI)**
The ability of an AI system to explain its decisions in terms understandable to humans. Critical for high-stakes applications in healthcare, finance, and justice.

---

## F

**FPO (Farmer Producer Organization)**
A collective of farmers organized as a legal entity to aggregate production, access markets, and negotiate collectively. A key distribution channel for agricultural technology in India.

**Federated Learning**
A machine learning approach where models are trained across decentralized devices without sharing raw data — the data stays on device; only model updates are shared.

---

## G

**Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping)**
A visualization technique that highlights the regions of an image most influential in a neural network's classification decision. Used for explainability.

---

## I

**Inference**
The process of using a trained machine learning model to make predictions on new data. Contrast with *training*, which is the process of building the model.

**IoT (Internet of Things)**
A network of physical devices embedded with sensors and connectivity to collect and exchange data.

**IUU Fishing (Illegal, Unreported, and Unregulated)**
Fishing that occurs outside the law or in contravention of conservation measures. Costs the global economy $23B annually.

---

## L

**Latency**
The delay between an input and the corresponding output in a computing system. Real-time applications require low latency (< 100ms). Cloud AI typically has 200ms–2000ms latency.

**LoRa (Long Range)**
A wireless communication protocol designed for long-range, low-power transmission of small data packets. Ideal for remote IoT and edge AI deployments.

**LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)**
A sensor that uses laser pulses to measure distances and create 3D maps of the environment. Used in autonomous vehicles, robotics, and environmental monitoring.

---

## M

**Model Drift**
The degradation of an AI model's performance over time as the real-world data distribution shifts away from the training data. A key challenge in long-deployed edge systems.

**Multi-sensor Fusion**
The integration of data from multiple sensor types (camera, lidar, thermal, acoustic) to produce a richer and more reliable understanding than any single sensor provides.

---

## N

**NDRF (National Disaster Response Force)**
India's specialized force for disaster response, with 16 battalions trained for flood, earthquake, chemical, and biological disaster response.

**NHM (National Health Mission)**
India's flagship public health program, providing healthcare funding and infrastructure to states — a key procurement channel for healthcare AI products.

---

## O

**OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)**
A manufacturing metric measuring the percentage of manufacturing time that is truly productive. World-class OEE is 85%; most Indian SMEs operate at 40–60%.

**On-Device AI**
See *Edge AI*. Intelligence that runs on the physical device rather than the cloud.

**OTA (Over-the-Air)**
The ability to update firmware, software, or AI models on deployed devices wirelessly, without physical access.

---

## P

**PPM (Parts Per Million)**
A measure of defect rate in manufacturing. A defect rate of 1,000 PPM means 1 in every 1,000 units is defective. Six Sigma quality = 3.4 PPM.

**Precision Agriculture**
A farming approach that uses data and technology to manage crop inputs (water, fertilizer, pesticide) with high spatial and temporal precision, reducing waste and improving yield.

**Predictive Maintenance**
Using sensor data and AI to predict when equipment will fail, enabling maintenance before failure occurs. Contrast with *reactive maintenance* (fix it when it breaks).

---

## Q

**Quantization**
A technique for reducing the computational requirements of AI models by representing weights with lower precision (e.g., INT8 instead of FP32). Essential for deploying models on power-constrained edge chips.

---

## R

**RPWD Act (Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act)**
India's 2016 legislation mandating accessibility and inclusion for persons with disabilities across education, employment, and public services.

**RSI (Retained Surgical Instrument)**
A surgical instrument, sponge, or foreign body accidentally left inside a patient after surgery. Causes serious complications and is entirely preventable.

---

## S

**SaMD (Software as a Medical Device)**
AI/software that performs a medical function without being part of a physical medical device. Regulated by CDSCO in India, FDA in the US.

**Semantic Segmentation**
An AI technique that classifies every pixel in an image into a category — useful for crop analysis, road detection, and medical imaging.

**Solar-powered IoT**
Devices powered by photovoltaic cells, enabling indefinite deployment without grid infrastructure. Critical for remote agricultural, environmental, and disaster monitoring.

---

## T

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
The total revenue opportunity available if a product captured 100% of its target market. Used to size market opportunity.

**Thermal Camera**
A camera that detects infrared radiation (heat) rather than visible light. Enables night vision, temperature measurement, and detection through smoke or dust.

**TinyML**
The field of deploying machine learning models on extremely resource-constrained microcontrollers with kilobytes of memory. Beyond Borders focuses on slightly more powerful Edge AI chips, but TinyML techniques are adjacent.

---

## V

**Vertical**
In this repository, a vertical refers to an industry sector or application domain — Agriculture AI, Healthcare AI, Manufacturing AI, etc.

**Vision AI**
AI systems specialized for analyzing visual data — images, video, depth maps. The most active and impactful subfield of Edge AI for physical-world applications.

---

## W

**YOLO (You Only Look Once)**
A family of real-time object detection models widely used in Edge AI applications. Known for speed and accuracy on embedded hardware.
