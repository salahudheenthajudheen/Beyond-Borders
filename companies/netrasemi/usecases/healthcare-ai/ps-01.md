# PS-01: Point-of-Care Tuberculosis Screening in Rural Health Centers

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Healthcare AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Portable Chest X-Ray Analysis

---

## Problem Statement

Tuberculosis (TB) kills more people in India than any other infectious disease — over 220,000 deaths annually. India carries 26% of the global TB burden. Yet in rural areas where TB prevalence is highest, diagnosis requires either sending sputum samples to district labs (results in 2–7 days) or accessing digital X-ray readers available only in district hospitals. Community health centers and sub-centers — the first point of contact for millions of rural patients — have no AI-assisted diagnostic tools.

The result: patients with active TB go undiagnosed for weeks, infect family members and community contacts, and present to hospitals only when disease is advanced.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- 1.4 billion rural Indians whose primary healthcare is a community health center
- ASHA workers and ANMs (Auxiliary Nurse Midwives) doing TB contact tracing
- District TB officers managing notification and treatment tracking
- Rural patients who cannot travel repeatedly to district hospitals

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Every undiagnosed TB patient infects 10–15 people annually
- Late-stage TB is 3x more expensive to treat and has significantly lower success rates
- Drug-resistant TB (MDR-TB) is rising, partly because of incomplete treatment from late diagnosis
- India has committed to TB elimination by 2025 — this gap is a direct barrier

---

## Proposed Solution

A portable edge AI device — ruggedized tablet form factor with a Netrasemi chip — that connects to a low-cost portable digital X-ray unit. The device runs a pre-trained deep learning model for TB detection on chest X-rays entirely offline. ASHA workers and PHC nurses receive an instant AI-assisted reading: probability of TB, abnormality heatmap, and recommended action (refer/monitor/clear).

**Key features:**
- Analyzes chest X-ray in < 5 seconds, offline
- Provides confidence score and visual explanation (Grad-CAM heatmap)
- Flags high-risk cases for immediate doctor referral via SMS
- Stores cases locally with encrypted patient ID; syncs to national TB registry when connected
- Works on battery; portable enough for camp/outreach settings

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Rural health centers and outreach camps have no reliable internet
- X-ray images (2–5MB each) cannot be transmitted on 2G/EDGE networks in acceptable time
- Patient data (X-rays, diagnoses) is highly sensitive — must not leave the facility without consent
- Real-time screening in a camp setting requires instant results, not a 24-hour turnaround

---

## Desirability

Healthcare workers want tools that extend their diagnostic capability without requiring specialist knowledge. District TB officers want higher case detection rates. Patients want answers the same day. The Ministry of Health has explicitly called for AI tools in TB screening under the National TB Elimination Programme.

**Desirability Score: 10/10**

---

## Feasibility

Deep learning models for TB detection on chest X-rays (such as CheXNet, TorchXRayVision) achieve radiologist-level sensitivity. These models can be quantized and deployed on an Edge AI chip. The hardware pairing with portable X-ray (e.g., Carestream, MinXray) is straightforward. Key challenge: model validation on India-specific X-ray datasets and regulatory clearance from CDSCO.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**

---

## Viability

**Revenue models:**
- Government procurement via National Health Mission (NHM) and Ayushman Bharat
- PPP (public-private partnership) with district health authorities
- Licensing to NGOs (PATH, MSF, Doctors Without Borders) for global deployment
- Per-reading fee via telemedicine platforms connecting rural X-rays to radiologists

**Viability Score: 8/10**

---

## Market Opportunity

- **TAM:** Global TB diagnostics market — $3.2B by 2027
- **SAM:** India rural primary healthcare AI diagnostics — $400M
- **Key Geographies:** India, Indonesia, Philippines, Nigeria, South Africa (high TB burden countries)

---

## Possible MVP

A Netrasemi-based edge device pre-loaded with a validated TB detection model, integrated with one compatible portable X-ray unit, piloted in 5 PHCs in a high-burden district over 6 months. Track: sensitivity, specificity, case detection uplift vs. control group.

**Timeline:** 16 weeks to device prototype; 6 months clinical validation

---

## Data Required

| Data Type | Source | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| Chest X-ray images with TB labels | NIH ChestX-ray14, India hospital datasets | Very High |
| India-specific X-ray quality variation data | State-level NHM radiology data | Very High |
| Clinical outcome data | RNTCP patient records | Very High |
| ASHA/ANM workflow data | Field observation | Low |

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Regulatory approval (CDSCO Medical Device) | High | Engage CDSCO early; pursue SaMD (Software as Medical Device) pathway |
| Model bias on low-quality rural X-rays | High | Train on India-specific low-quality X-ray data; build quality filter |
| Over-reliance on AI (de-skilling of nurses) | Medium | Design as decision support, not replacement; mandatory doctor sign-off for referrals |
| Patient data privacy | High | On-device encryption; no patient-identifiable data in cloud |
