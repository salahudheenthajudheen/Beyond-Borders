# PS-03: Post-Harvest Quality Grading and Rejection at the Farm Gate

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Agriculture AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Conveyor/Sorting Integration

---

## Problem Statement

Farmers in India lose 30–40% of the value of their harvest not in the field, but after it — during post-harvest handling, transportation, and grading. The grading of produce (size, color, surface defects, ripeness) is done manually by middlemen at mandis (wholesale markets), and farmers have no visibility or leverage in this process. Produce that could command a premium price is lumped into lower grades due to inconsistent, subjective, and often corrupt manual grading. Small farmers cannot afford or access automated sorting machines designed for large packhouses.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Vegetable and fruit farmers supplying fresh produce markets (tomato, mango, potato, onion, apple)
- Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) aggregating produce from multiple small farms
- Cold chain logistics companies
- Retailers and exporters requiring consistent quality

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India's post-harvest losses total $14B annually — one of the highest in the world
- Farmers receive 20–30% of the retail price; unfair grading suppresses even that
- Export rejection rates for Indian produce are high due to inconsistent quality standards
- Lack of objective grading prevents farm-level price discovery

---

## Proposed Solution

A portable, self-contained edge AI grading unit — the size of a large toolbox — that FPOs and village aggregation centers can deploy at the farm gate. Produce moves through on a short conveyor belt; the Netrasemi-powered vision system captures multi-angle images and grades each unit in real time (grade A/B/C, defect type, ripeness index). The unit generates a tamper-proof grading certificate the farmer can take to market or use for insurance claims.

**Key features:**
- Grade 5–10 units of produce per second
- Works on 6–12 crop types with a modular model library
- No internet needed; outputs printable/QR grading certificate
- Connects to FPO management software via local WiFi
- Portable: runs on 12V power, fits in a vehicle

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Farm gates and aggregation centers have no internet connectivity
- Real-time grading (seconds per item) is impossible with cloud round-trips
- Sensitive pricing and volume data must stay within the FPO ecosystem
- Low-cost device requires efficient, dedicated edge compute rather than a GPU workstation

---

## Desirability

Farmers want objective, irrefutable grading that reflects the true quality of their produce. FPOs want a tool that enables them to negotiate with buyers from a position of data. Exporters and retailers want consistent quality standards. Government wants to reduce post-harvest losses and increase farmer income.

**Desirability Score: 9/10**

---

## Feasibility

Computer vision for produce grading is a mature research area. Models for tomato, mango, potato, and apple grading exist. The hardware (short conveyor, multi-angle camera array, lighting box, edge compute unit) is straightforward to engineer. Key challenge: building an India-specific dataset covering local varieties (Alphonso mango vs. Kesar vs. Langra), and achieving sub-2% error rate in real grading conditions.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

---

## Viability

**Revenue models:**
- Unit lease to FPOs: ₹3,000–5,000/month per unit
- Per-unit grading fee: ₹0.10–0.50 per item graded (high-volume = high revenue)
- Grading certificate as a product: integrated with crop insurance (graded lots have lower insurance premiums)
- Data play: grading data aggregated across FPOs = price forecasting intelligence sold to commodity traders and retailers

**Viability Score: 9/10**

---

## Market Opportunity

- **TAM:** India post-harvest loss reduction technology — $2B+ addressable
- **SAM:** FPO-level grading and aggregation technology — $300M by 2027
- **Key Geographies:** India (Maharashtra, UP, AP, Karnataka, Himachal), South Asia, East Africa

---

## Possible MVP

A grading system for 3 crops (tomato, mango, potato), detecting 5 defect types each, deployed with 3 FPOs across one harvest season. Track: grading throughput, farmer income change, buyer adoption of grading certificates.

**Timeline:** 14 weeks to prototype, 2 harvest cycles for validation

## Data Required

| Data Type | Source | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| Produce images across grades and defects | Field collection at mandis/FPOs | Low |
| Grading standards per crop | APEDA, AGMARK, exporters | Low |
| Price-quality correlation data | Mandi price data (AgMarkNet) | Low |
| FPO transaction data | FPO management software | High |

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Model accuracy for local crop varieties | High | Extensive India-specific dataset collection |
| Middlemen resistance (disrupts their grading power) | High | Go direct to FPOs; target export-oriented FPOs first |
| Conveyor maintenance in dusty environments | Medium | Robust industrial-grade mechanical design |
| Farmer trust in machine grading vs. human | Medium | Transparent display; dispute resolution process |
