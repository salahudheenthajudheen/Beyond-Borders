# PS-02: Autonomous Inspection Robot for Underground Utility Infrastructure

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Robotics AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Camera + Gas Sensor + Acoustic NDT

---

## Problem Statement

India has 1.5+ million kilometers of underground water pipes, sewer lines, telecom ducts, and gas pipelines that need periodic inspection for leaks, blockages, cracks, and corrosion. Today, inspection requires manual entry into confined spaces — one of the most dangerous occupations in India. 350+ sewer workers die every year in India from toxic gas exposure, drowning, and asphyxiation inside manholes. Despite a Supreme Court order banning manual scavenging in 2014, the practice continues because no affordable automated alternative exists for narrow-diameter pipes and complex sewer networks.

Large industrial pipeline inspection robots (>$100,000) exist but are designed for wide-diameter industrial pipes and are not suitable for India's municipal sewer and water network geometry.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Municipal corporations and urban local bodies managing sewage networks
- Water and gas utility companies doing pipeline maintenance
- Telecom operators managing underground duct networks
- Sanitation workers who currently risk their lives for manual inspection
- Urban planners trying to modernize aging infrastructure

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Manual scavenging deaths are a human rights crisis — each death is entirely preventable
- Undetected sewer blockages cause urban flooding during monsoon (Mumbai, Chennai experience this annually)
- Undetected water pipe leaks waste 40–60% of treated water in Indian cities (non-revenue water loss)
- Gas pipeline leaks are catastrophic explosion risks in dense urban environments

---

## Proposed Solution

A compact autonomous inspection robot — Netrasemi chip + 360° camera + gas sensor array (H2S, CH4, CO, O2) + acoustic pipe wall thickness sensor — designed to navigate 15–30cm diameter pipes without human entry. The robot self-propels through the pipe, continuously mapping the interior, detecting defects (cracks, corrosion, root intrusion, blockages), measuring gas concentrations, and estimating structural integrity. All processing happens on the Netrasemi edge chip; the robot surfaces with a complete inspection report, defect map, and repair priority ranking. No cloud required.

**Key features:**
- Navigates pipes from 15cm to 60cm diameter
- Self-recovers from partial blockages (reverse drive, variable geometry chassis)
- 360° wall inspection at 1mm resolution
- Gas hazard detection with real-time alert to operator
- Acoustic wall thickness measurement (non-destructive)
- Maps entire pipe network with GPS-correlated defect locations
- Inspection report generated onboard; displays on operator tablet via local WiFi
- 2-hour battery life; 500m inspection range per charge

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Underground pipe environments have no wireless connectivity
- Real-time navigation in unstructured pipe geometry requires millisecond decision-making
- Camera + sensor data cannot be tethered (cable drag in long pipe runs)
- Gas detection alerts must trigger robot stop/reverse instantly — cloud latency is unacceptable

## Feasibility

Pipeline inspection robots for large-diameter pipes are commercial products. Miniaturizing to 15cm diameter with full autonomy is a significant engineering challenge. Netrasemi's edge compute enables on-device navigation; the mechanical design (locomotion, sealing, power management in a 15cm chassis) is the primary engineering constraint.

**Feasibility Score: 6/10**
