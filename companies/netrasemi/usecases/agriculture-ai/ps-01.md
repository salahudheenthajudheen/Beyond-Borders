# PS-01: Real-Time Crop Disease Detection for Smallholder Farmers

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Agriculture AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Portable Camera Device

---

## Problem Statement

Smallholder farmers in India and Southeast Asia lose 20–40% of their crop yield annually to diseases and pests that go undetected until visible damage has spread across the field. By the time a farmer notices wilting leaves, discoloration, or lesions, the infection has often propagated beyond cost-effective treatment.

Existing solutions require smartphone connectivity (unreliable in rural areas), expensive agronomist visits, or laboratory sample analysis that takes days. Farmers act too late, use broad-spectrum pesticides as a precaution (increasing costs and environmental damage), or simply absorb the loss.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- 100+ million smallholder farmers in India alone
- Subsistence and semi-commercial farmers across South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Sub-Saharan Africa
- Agricultural extension officers responsible for hundreds of farmers per officer

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India loses ~₹50,000 crore (~$6B) annually to crop diseases
- Smallholder farmers have no economic buffer — a single bad season pushes families into debt
- Overuse of pesticides degrades soil health, pollutes water, and increases input costs
- Climate change is accelerating the spread of new disease strains

---

## Proposed Solution

A low-cost, handheld edge AI device — powered by a Netrasemi chip — that farmers carry into the field. The device captures images of leaves, stems, and soil and runs an on-device vision model trained to identify 50+ crop diseases across rice, wheat, maize, tomato, and cotton. It provides an instant diagnosis with recommended action — in the local language, with no internet connection required.

**Key features:**
- Identifies disease type, severity, and spread
- Recommends targeted (not broad-spectrum) treatment
- Works offline in remote fields
- Sync data to a central agronomist dashboard when connectivity is available
- Solar-powered charging cradle for field stations

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Fields are often 5–20km from the nearest reliable internet connection
- Cloud latency of 2–5 seconds is unusable during in-field diagnosis workflows
- Farmer training data (images, diagnoses, outcomes) is sensitive IP — keeping it on-device prevents data leakage to agribusiness competitors
- Battery-constrained device cannot sustain constant data transmission

---

## Desirability

Farmers want to know what's wrong with their crop — fast, cheaply, without waiting for an expert. Early disease detection directly translates to higher yield and income. Extension officers want scalable tools they can distribute without training overhead. Agribusinesses want precision data on disease spread across geographies.

**Desirability Score: 9/10**

---

## Feasibility

Computer vision models for major crop diseases (rice blast, wheat rust, tomato blight, etc.) already exist in research form. Datasets like PlantVillage provide training data. Netrasemi chips can run quantized CNN models for image classification in real time. The hardware integration (camera + chip + battery + enclosure) is well within current engineering capability.

Key challenge: building a robust, India-conditions dataset (variable lighting, soil backgrounds, crop varieties) and continuous model updates.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

---

## Viability

Government procurement and CSR funding from agribusiness provide early non-dilutive revenue.

**Viability Score: 8/10**

## Market Opportunity

- **TAM:** $6B+ annual crop loss reduction market in India alone

---

## Possible MVP

A mobile camera attachment + Netrasemi edge AI module trained on 5 crops (rice, wheat, tomato, cotton, maize) across 20 most common diseases. Works offline. Gives diagnosis + recommendation in 3 seconds. Tested with 50 farmers across 3 districts in one crop cycle.

**Timeline:** 12 weeks to working prototype, 6 months to field trial

## Data Required

| Data Type | Source | Sensitivity |

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Model accuracy in field conditions | High | Robust field dataset collection; active learning loop |
| Low farmer digital literacy | Medium | Voice output; local language UI; community health worker distribution |
| Competition from smartphone apps | Medium | Offline capability + lower cost device is key differentiator |
| Regulatory (pesticide recommendations) | Low | Partner with licensed agronomists; provide information, not prescription |
