# PS-02: Edge AI Security and Anomaly Detection for University Campus Safety

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Smart Campus AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Distributed Camera Network

---

## Problem Statement

University and college campuses in India house 43 million students, many of them away from home for the first time. Campus safety incidents — ragging, sexual harassment, unauthorized entry, late-night perimeter breaches — are persistent and underreported. Security is typically provided by a small guard team monitoring a CCTV control room with 20–100 camera feeds, which is cognitively impossible to watch continuously. Incidents are reviewed after they occur, not prevented in real time.

Female students in residential campuses have particularly acute safety concerns — and manual guard monitoring creates its own privacy and harassment issues.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Students living in campus hostels (especially women's hostels)
- Campus security and administration teams
- Parents sending children to residential universities for the first time
- University management facing UGC scrutiny on campus safety compliance
- Anti-ragging committees required under UGC regulation

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Ragging-related deaths and suicides average 25–30 per year in Indian colleges
- Sexual harassment on campuses is severely underreported due to fear and lack of evidence
- Unauthorized entry by external visitors causes theft, harassment, and assault
- Universities face legal and reputational consequences from safety failures

---

## Proposed Solution

A campus-wide edge AI safety network — Netrasemi chips in distributed camera nodes — that provides real-time anomaly detection rather than passive recording. The system detects: (1) perimeter breaches (unauthorized entry after hours), (2) unusual gathering patterns (large group formations associated with ragging incidents), (3) individual in distress (person lying on ground, running in panic, surrounded by group), (4) loitering in sensitive zones (women's hostel perimeter, parking lots at night). Privacy-preserving: behavioral anomaly detection only, no facial recognition, no identity tracking.

**Key features:**
- Behavioral anomaly detection across 50+ camera nodes
- Zone-specific rules: different sensitivity for hostel areas, labs, sports grounds
- Instant alert to security control room + on-call security officer's phone
- Privacy-first: only event clips stored; full video auto-deleted after 72 hours
- Night operation via low-light and thermal cameras
- Anti-ragging pattern detection: large group surrounding smaller group in isolated area
- Integration with campus emergency call boxes

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Campus networks cannot stream 50+ camera feeds to cloud continuously
- Real-time anomaly detection needs < 3 second alert latency — cloud processing fails this
- Student behavioral data is legally protected under data privacy law
- Cost-effective per-camera deployment requires edge compute, not cloud GPU

## Feasibility

Person detection, group activity recognition, and behavioral anomaly detection are maturing rapidly. Running these on Netrasemi chips in a distributed architecture is feasible. Key challenge: reducing false alarms (students studying in groups at night ≠ ragging incident) through context-aware models.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Student surveillance backlash | High | Strong privacy policy; student body involvement in system design |
| False alarms causing unnecessary security incidents | High | Tiered alert system; high confidence threshold for active alerts |
| Security staff misusing access to camera footage | Medium | Strict access controls; audit logs; footage auto-deletion |
| Ragging detection accuracy in ambiguous situations | High | Conservative detection; human review for all alerts before response |
