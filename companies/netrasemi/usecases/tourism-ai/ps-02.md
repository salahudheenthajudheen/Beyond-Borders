# PS-02: Wildlife Monitoring and Anti-Poaching Intelligence in National Parks

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Tourism AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Solar Camera Trap Network

---

## Problem Statement

India's national parks and wildlife sanctuaries host some of the world's most endangered species — Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, Indian rhinos, snow leopards. Wildlife monitoring depends on camera traps that generate thousands of images daily — 90% of which are empty frames (false triggers from wind, light). Rangers must manually review 500–2000 images per day per camera network, consuming hours that could be spent on actual patrol. Meanwhile, poachers who know camera locations and patrol patterns continue to exploit blind spots.

At the same time, eco-tourists visit 500+ protected areas expecting sighting information and guided experiences that park staff cannot provide at scale due to limited ranger resources.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Forest department rangers and wildlife managers
- Park ecologists and researchers conducting animal population studies
- Wildlife tourism operators running safari experiences
- Anti-poaching units and TRAFFIC (wildlife trade monitoring)
- Tourists and wildlife photographers wanting real sighting intelligence

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India has 50+ tiger reserves; tiger population recovery depends on continuous monitoring
- Poaching of rhinos (horn), elephants (ivory), and tigers (skins) is a billion-dollar criminal market
- Eco-tourism is a $6B+ sector in India — improving sighting intelligence directly improves operator revenues
- Climate change is causing wildlife range shifts that require continuous monitoring to track

---

## Proposed Solution

A solar-powered edge AI camera trap — Netrasemi chip + high-resolution camera + thermal sensor — that processes every captured image on the device. The device distinguishes animals from empty frames in real time, classifies species and individual animal identity, and transmits only meaningful events (specific species detected, suspected poacher vehicle, unknown human in core zone) via LoRa mesh network to a ranger control room.

**Key features:**
- Species classification for 30+ Indian wildlife species
- Individual animal identification (tiger stripe pattern, elephant ear patterns)
- Human intrusion detection in no-entry core zones
- Vehicle detection for night-time poacher vehicle tracking
- LoRa mesh transmission: only event notifications (< 1KB), not images
- 90-day autonomous deployment on solar + battery
- Real-time sighting intelligence dashboard for rangers and eco-tourism operators

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Remote forest locations have no connectivity of any kind
- Camera traps generate TB of data per month — impossible to transmit
- Real-time poacher detection requires on-device processing (not overnight batch review)
- Low-power operation on solar is essential for remote, indefinite deployment

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Device theft/destruction by poachers | Medium | Camouflage housing; decoy devices; GPS tracking |
| False alarms triggering unnecessary ranger deployment | High | High-confidence threshold for alert transmission; two-camera corroboration |
| LoRa mesh coverage in dense forest | Medium | Network planning; mesh relay nodes every 3km |
| Animal welfare: flash triggers and stress | Low | IR-only illumination; passive thermal sensing |
