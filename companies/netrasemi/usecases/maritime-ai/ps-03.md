# PS-03: Edge AI for Real-Time Hull and Underwater Structure Inspection

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Maritime AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Underwater ROV + Vision + Acoustic Sensing

---

## Problem Statement

Ship hull maintenance and underwater structure inspection (ports, jetties, offshore platforms, sea cables) is expensive, dangerous, and infrequent. Divers conducting underwater inspections face safety risks and can cover only small areas per dive. Drydock hull inspections require pulling vessels out of service for weeks at $100,000–$500,000 per event. As a result, hull corrosion, biofouling, and structural cracks often go undetected between inspections — leading to catastrophic failures, fuel inefficiency from fouled hulls (biofouling increases fuel consumption 10–15%), and cargo loss.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Ship owners and operators managing fleet maintenance budgets
- Port infrastructure managers responsible for jetty and quay integrity
- Offshore platform operators (oil, gas, wind)
- Marine insurance underwriters
- Navy and Coast Guard managing vessel readiness

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Hull corrosion failures cause $50B+ in maritime losses annually
- A 10% increase in hull roughness from biofouling costs $1M+ in additional fuel per year for a large vessel
- Port infrastructure failures (jetty collapse) cause catastrophic cargo and life loss
- Environmental regulations increasingly require proof of hull integrity and biofouling management

---

## Proposed Solution

A small, tethered underwater ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle) carrying a Netrasemi edge AI module, high-resolution camera, and acoustic thickness sensor. The ROV conducts autonomous hull-following inspection runs, while the Netrasemi chip processes video in real time to detect and classify: corrosion spots, coating failures, biofouling density, cracks, dents, and cathodic protection anode depletion. The inspection report is generated onboard the ROV — no cloud required — with precise GPS/position tags for each detected anomaly.

**Key features:**
- Autonomous hull-following navigation (no pilot required for straight runs)
- Real-time defect detection and classification at 4K resolution
- Acoustic thickness measurement for corrosion depth estimation
- Generates inspection report and 3D hull defect map during the dive
- Fits through a 30cm port access hatch; deployable from any vessel
- Works in turbid water (poor visibility) with illumination array

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Underwater environments have no wireless connectivity — tether bandwidth is limited to a few Mbps
- 4K video at 30fps cannot be transmitted in real time over a tether for remote cloud processing
- Real-time defect detection enables the ROV to pause and acquire additional detail images on defects found mid-inspection
- Onboard processing means the inspection report is ready immediately when the ROV surfaces

## Feasibility

Underwater vehicle technology is established. Computer vision for corrosion detection is an active research area with published benchmarks. Acoustic thickness sensors are commercially available. The key challenges are: robust visual model performance in turbid water, and ROV navigation stability for consistent image capture.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**

## Possible MVP

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Turbid water degrading vision model | High | Model training on low-visibility images; structured lighting design |
| ROV current stability in port waters | Medium | Thruster configuration and stabilization software |

| Tether management in complex hull geometries | Medium | Auto-tether management system; human takeover for complex areas |
