# Vertical: Agriculture AI

> *Feeding 8 billion people with less land, less water, and less waste — through intelligence at the field edge.*

---

## Overview

Agriculture AI is the application of artificial intelligence to farming, food production, and agri-supply chains. The intersection of Edge AI with agriculture is particularly powerful because the most important agricultural decisions — irrigation, pest control, harvest timing, disease management — happen at the field level, far from cloud connectivity.

India alone has 140 million farm holdings, 600+ million people dependent on agriculture, and some of the world's most acute food security, water scarcity, and climate resilience challenges. Agriculture AI at the edge is not optional for India's future — it is essential.

---

## Core Problem Areas

- **Crop disease and pest detection** — Early, accurate, in-field detection
- **Precision irrigation** — Matching water delivery to actual crop need
- **Post-harvest quality** — Objective grading and loss reduction
- **Soil health intelligence** — Continuous, real-time soil monitoring
- **Weather and microclimate sensing** — Hyper-local prediction for field decisions
- **Livestock monitoring** — Health, behavior, and productivity tracking
- **Supply chain traceability** — From farm to fork, with verifiable data

---

## Why Edge AI in Agriculture?

| Challenge | Why Cloud AI Fails | Why Edge AI Works |
|---|---|---|
| Remote fields | No connectivity | Fully offline operation |
| Real-time decisions | Latency too high | Millisecond inference |
| Data sensitivity | Farmer IP at risk | Data stays on device |
| Cost at scale | Cloud fees unsustainable | One-time hardware cost |
| Power constraints | Battery cannot sustain transmission | Low-power chip |
