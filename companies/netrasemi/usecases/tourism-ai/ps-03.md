# PS-03: Smart Crowd Management and Safety Intelligence at Religious and Cultural Mass Events

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Tourism AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Distributed Camera Network

---

## Problem Statement

India hosts the world's largest human gatherings — Kumbh Mela (50–100 million attendees), Char Dham Yatra, Tirupati Balaji, Sabrimala, Pushkar Fair. Crowd crushes at these events kill 50–200+ people per event cycle, with hundreds injured. Event organizers and state police manage crowd flow using human judgment, manual headcounts, and radio coordination — all of which fail catastrophically under the surge dynamics that cause stampedes.

The 2022 Amritsar, 2013 Madhya Pradesh, and 2008 Jodhpur stampedes each killed 100–250 people. Every year, the same infrastructure faces the same problem.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- State police and district administration managing event security
- Shrine management boards (Tirupati, Shirdi, Vaishno Devi)
- Event organizers for festivals and fairs
- NDMA (National Disaster Management Authority) for crowd safety standards
- Pilgrims and devotees who attend these events

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Crowd crush events are entirely predictable and preventable with real-time density intelligence
- India's religious tourism sector generates ₹2+ lakh crore annually — safety failures threaten the entire sector
- Post-incident liability, criminal prosecution of officials, and reputational damage to shrine management are severe
- The technology to prevent these deaths exists — it is only the last-mile deployment that is missing

---

## Proposed Solution

A distributed edge AI camera network — Netrasemi chips embedded in ruggedized camera nodes deployed along procession routes, entry queues, and critical chokepoints — that provides real-time crowd density mapping, flow velocity measurement, and early surge detection. When density crosses a danger threshold (>6 people/m²) or flow velocity reverses (a key precursor to crowd crush), the system alerts control room operators and triggers automatic queue gate closures and PA announcements — before the surge becomes a crush.

**Key features:**
- Real-time crowd density heatmap (people per m² across 50–200 camera zones)
- Directional flow measurement and reverse-flow early warning
- Crush risk score updated every 5 seconds per zone
- Automatic alert escalation: operators → incident commander → emergency services
- Privacy-preserving: head counts only, no face recognition, no identity data
- Deployable in temporary infrastructure (pillar-mounted cameras) for events without permanent structure
- Battery-powered nodes with 72-hour autonomy

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Kumbh and Char Dham sites have no reliable internet infrastructure
- Network congestion from 10+ million mobile users makes cloud-dependent systems unreliable during peak events
- Crowd density analysis of video from 200 cameras simultaneously requires distributed edge compute
- Alert latency must be < 5 seconds — a 30-second cloud lag could be the difference between a warning and a disaster

## Desirability

Event administrators and police want systems they can point to as active safety measures. State governments want to avoid the political fallout of preventable mass casualty events. Shrine management boards want to protect pilgrims and maintain reputation. The NDMA has explicitly called for technology solutions in mass gathering safety.

**Desirability Score: 10/10**

---

## Feasibility

Crowd density estimation via overhead camera vision is a well-studied problem (ShanghaiTech dataset, JHU-Crowd). Edge deployment is feasible. The main challenge is: (1) camera placement design for the irregular, temporary infrastructure of pilgrimage events, and (2) creating an alert interface that overwhelmed control room operators can actually use under pressure.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**
