# PS-02: Autonomous Irrigation Intelligence for Water-Scarce Farming Regions

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Agriculture AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Multi-Sensor Array + Actuator Control

---

## Problem Statement

Water scarcity is one of the defining agricultural crises of the 21st century. India uses 78% of its total freshwater for agriculture, and 60% of that is wasted through inefficient, schedule-based flood irrigation that does not account for actual crop water needs. Farmers irrigate on fixed schedules regardless of soil moisture, rainfall, or crop growth stage — because they lack real-time soil and crop intelligence at the field level.

Existing smart irrigation systems require cloud connectivity and are designed for large commercial farms. Smallholder farmers — who operate 1–5 acre plots — cannot afford or maintain these systems, and have no connectivity in their fields.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- 80+ million smallholder farmers dependent on groundwater irrigation in India
- Irrigation departments managing canal and reservoir allocations
- Agri-input companies selling fertilizers and water-soluble nutrients
- Farmers in water-stressed regions: Punjab, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Telangana, Tamil Nadu

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India's groundwater table is falling by 0.3m per year in the most irrigated states
- Over-irrigation causes soil salinization and nutrient leaching, degrading long-term land productivity
- Under-irrigation during critical crop growth stages causes 15–30% yield loss
- Water conflicts between farmers are a significant source of rural violence

---

## Proposed Solution

A solar-powered, edge AI field station containing a Netrasemi chip, soil moisture sensors (at multiple depths), ambient temperature/humidity sensors, a camera for crop canopy analysis, and a valve controller for drip/sprinkler systems. The device runs a crop-specific water stress model locally, deciding when and how much to irrigate without any cloud dependency.

**Key features:**
- Measures real soil moisture at root depth, not just surface
- Correlates crop canopy thermal signature with water stress (via thermal camera)
- Automatically controls irrigation valve — no farmer intervention needed
- Communicates irrigation events and water usage to a farm dashboard via SMS/LoRa when available
- Serves 1–2 acres per unit; daisy-chainable for larger farms

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Field locations have no internet; even SMS connectivity is intermittent
- Irrigation decisions must be made in minutes, not hours — crop stress can escalate rapidly in summer heat
- Multi-sensor fusion (soil + thermal + environmental) is computationally intensive — cloud round-trip is impractical
- Solar power budget limits the device to low-power edge compute only

---

## Desirability

Farmers want to reduce water costs (pumping electricity bills are their second-largest input cost) and protect their crop during critical growth stages. Irrigation departments want data on actual water usage. NGOs and government schemes focused on water conservation are actively seeking technology solutions.

**Desirability Score: 9/10**

---

## Feasibility

Soil moisture sensors, thermal cameras, and valve actuators are commercially available. Crop water stress models (based on CWSI — Crop Water Stress Index) are well-researched. Edge AI fusion of these signals on a Netrasemi chip is feasible. The primary engineering challenge is robust enclosure design for field deployment and reliable valve control across different irrigation infrastructure types.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**

---

## Possible MVP

A single-sensor (soil moisture + temperature) edge controller with automatic valve control for drip irrigation, working on a simple rule-based AI model. Deployed on 20 farms across 2 crop cycles. Track: water saved, yield change, farmer acceptance.

**Timeline:** 10 weeks prototype, 4 months field validation

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Sensor calibration drift in field | Medium | Annual maintenance visit; self-calibration routines |
| Farmer trust in automated valve control | High | Manual override; transparent alerts; farmer training |
| Subsidy delays impacting unit economics | Medium | Direct-to-FPO sales channel to aggregate demand |
| Vandalism or theft in open fields | Low | Low-profile enclosure; community ownership model |
