# PS-03: Forest Fire Detection and Spread Prediction in Remote Forest Areas

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Disaster Management AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Thermal Camera + Environmental Sensor + Solar Tower Node

---

## Problem Statement

India loses 10+ million hectares of forest to fire annually, with 90% of fires going undetected until they are already large and uncontrollable. Fire lookout towers are understaffed; satellite detection has 3–6 hour latency and misses small fires in cloudy conditions; and forest department patrolling is insufficient for the scale of India's 71 million hectares of forest area. The window to suppress a forest fire effectively is the first 15–30 minutes — after which it doubles in area every 20 minutes under normal wind conditions.

Climate change is extending fire seasons and creating fire conditions across previously fire-safe forest types like the Shola grasslands and Northeast India's subtropical forests.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- State Forest Departments and Range Officers
- Fire-sensitive eco-systems: tiger reserves, elephant corridors, biodiversity hotspots
- Communities adjacent to forests (smoke, crop damage, displacement)
- Carbon credit project operators managing forest carbon stocks
- India's climate commitments (forest carbon sink targets under NDC)

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Forest fire releases carbon stored over decades, directly undermining India's climate commitments
- Each major fire season costs India's Forest Department ₹500 crore+ in suppression and replanting
- Biodiversity loss from uncontrolled forest fire is irreversible
- Human settlements adjacent to forests face life-threatening fire fronts with no warning

---

## Proposed Solution

A network of solar-powered edge AI fire detection towers — Netrasemi chip + 360° thermal pan-tilt camera + smoke optical sensor + wind/humidity/temperature sensor — deployed at 5–8km intervals in fire-prone forest divisions. Each tower detects fire signatures (thermal plume, smoke pattern) within its viewing radius using an on-device vision model, and triangulates fire location with adjacent towers. A fire spread prediction model running on the Netrasemi chip fuses weather data, terrain, and fuel load information to predict fire spread direction and speed for the next 30–60 minutes. Alerts are transmitted to Forest Department control rooms via LoRa + satellite.

**Key features:**
- Detects fire within 5km radius in < 2 minutes of ignition
- 24/7 monitoring: thermal camera works at night and through smoke haze
- Fire spread prediction: directional spread map updated every 5 minutes
- Multi-tower triangulation for precise fire location (50m accuracy)
- Automated alert to forest range officers with GPS location and spread prediction
- 45-day autonomous operation on solar + battery
- Connects to ISRO's Fire Watch portal when available

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Forests have no connectivity infrastructure
- Thermal and video data from 360° cameras cannot be transmitted over low-bandwidth LoRa/satellite links
- Real-time fire spread prediction must run locally — a 30-minute cloud batch cycle is useless
- Solar power budget limits compute to low-power edge architecture

Carbon project operators want proof of protected forest health. Communities want wildfire warnings before smoke reaches their village.

## Feasibility

Thermal fire detection from elevated cameras is proven technology. Wildfire spread modeling (FARSITE, FlamMap) is an established field. Edge deployment of thermal detection and simplified spread prediction models on Netrasemi chips is feasible. Key challenge: calibrating spread models for India's diverse forest and terrain types.

**Feasibility Score: 7/10**

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Tower damage from fires approaching detection range | Medium | Tower siting protocol (outside primary fire risk zone); fireproof enclosure |
| False alarms from sun glare, industrial smoke | High | Multi-sensor confirmation (thermal + smoke + weather conditions); confidence threshold |
| Camera obscuration from thick smoke | Medium | Thermal primary sensor is robust to smoke; optical sensor for early plume detection |
| State government procurement cycles | High | Pilot via WWF/WCS/carbon project operators first; use results for government tender |
