# PS-03: Autonomous Agricultural Robot for Weeding and Targeted Spraying

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Robotics AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Vision + Precision Actuator + Autonomous Navigation

---

## Problem Statement

Manual weeding is one of the most labor-intensive and costly agricultural operations — accounting for 20–30% of total crop production cost for smallholder farmers. Herbicide spraying, the most common alternative, is applied as a blanket treatment that kills beneficial soil organisms, creates herbicide-resistant weed populations, and contaminates groundwater. India's rural labor shortage is worsening as young people migrate to cities, driving up weeding labor costs by 40–60% in the last decade.

Precision weeding robots that identify individual weeds and eliminate them mechanically or with micro-dose herbicide exist commercially, but at $80,000–$150,000 — viable only for large corporate farms in the US and Europe.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Smallholder and mid-scale farmers growing row crops (rice, wheat, maize, soybean, cotton)
- Organic farmers who cannot use herbicides
- Farmer Producer Organizations managing pooled cultivation resources
- Agri-input companies facing market pressure as herbicide resistance increases
- Environmental regulators concerned about herbicide groundwater contamination

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Weeds reduce crop yield by 30–40% if uncontrolled; by 10–15% even with chemical control
- Herbicide-resistant super-weeds are spreading across India's major cropping zones
- Labor cost for manual weeding is projected to double by 2030 as rural labor supply contracts
- Herbicide runoff is a primary cause of groundwater contamination in agricultural districts

---

## Proposed Solution

A low-cost autonomous weeding robot — Netrasemi chip + downward-facing RGB camera + mechanical micro-weeding actuator — designed for inter-row and intra-row operation in vegetable, pulse, and row-crop fields. The robot navigates crop rows autonomously, identifying weeds vs. crop plants in real time using an on-device vision model, and either: (1) mechanically disrupts the weed root with a precision tiller, or (2) applies a micro-dose of herbicide (5–10ml) directly to the weed. 95% reduction in herbicide use per hectare compared to broadcast spraying.

**Key features:**
- Distinguishes 30+ weed species from 10+ crop types in varied growth stages
- < 0.5% crop damage rate (precision targeting)
- Navigates row widths from 25–80cm
- 6-hour battery life; 2 hectares per day coverage
- Fully autonomous; farmer sets boundary on a smartphone map
- Mechanical or chemical weeding mode (switchable)
- Priced for FPO shared ownership model (₹3–5 lakh vs. ₹60 lakh for imported alternatives)

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Fields have no connectivity; robot must navigate and classify plants entirely autonomously
- Weed vs. crop classification must happen in real time (200ms per detection at robot operating speed)
- Multi-terrain navigation (uneven soil, irrigation furrows, loose earth) requires continuous local decision-making
- Solar-charged battery budget requires energy-efficient edge AI, not a power-hungry GPU

---

## Desirability

Farmers want to reduce weeding costs without increasing herbicide use. Organic farmers want a non-chemical alternative to herbicides. FPOs want shared equipment that provides labor-equivalent service at lower cost. Agri-input companies want a precision herbicide delivery platform.

**Desirability Score: 9/10**

---

## Feasibility

Weed detection AI is an active research field with available datasets (DeepWeeds, WeedNet). Autonomous row navigation is well-studied. The mechanical actuator design for in-row weeding is the key engineering challenge. Achieving < 0.5% crop damage with the precision actuator requires tight integration between the vision model and actuator timing. Feasible but requires 18–24 months of serious engineering.

**Feasibility Score: 6/10**

---

- Government subsidy eligibility: SMAM (Sub Mission on Agricultural Mechanization) covers 25–50% of cost

**Viability Score: 7/10**

## Possible MVP

A robot that navigates a straight crop row and identifies weeds (without mechanical action) with > 90% accuracy across 3 crop types. Demonstrated in a field trial across 5 farmers over one crop season.

**Timeline:** 16 weeks to navigation + vision prototype, 6 months crop season trial

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Crop damage from imprecise actuator | High | Conservative accuracy threshold before actuation; soft actuator design |
| Soil variability affecting navigation | High | All-terrain wheel design; IMU-assisted navigation |
| Model accuracy on local weed varieties not in training data | High | India-specific weed dataset collection; active learning |
| Robot tipping on uneven terrain | Medium | Low center of gravity design; slope detection and stop |
