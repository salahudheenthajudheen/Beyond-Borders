# PS-01: Offline AI Guide for Heritage Sites in Low-Connectivity Destinations

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Tourism AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Vision + NLP + Audio Output

---

## Problem Statement

India has 3,690 centrally protected monuments (ASI), thousands of state heritage sites, and some of the world's most important cultural and natural destinations. Yet 90% of these sites have no professional guide services, no multilingual interpretation, and no digital interactivity. Tourists — domestic and international — arrive at Hampi, Ajanta, Rani ki Vav, or Sundarbans and encounter either nothing or a single-language signboard.

Smartphone audio guides require reliable internet connectivity, which is absent at most remote heritage sites. Hiring a human guide costs $20–50 and requires advance booking that most domestic tourists don't do.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- 2 billion+ annual domestic tourists in India visiting heritage and nature sites
- International tourists wanting multilingual interpretation in remote sites
- ASI and state tourism departments with limited budget for human guides
- Site managers wanting to provide quality experiences without increasing staff

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Poor tourist experience at heritage sites reduces dwell time, spending, and return visits
- Cultural misinterpretation damages heritage sites (tourists unaware of rules)
- India's tourism GDP is $250B; improving experience quality at tier-2 sites could unlock significant growth
- Heritage sites with low visitor satisfaction fail to generate funds for their own conservation

---

## Proposed Solution

A ruggedized edge AI kiosk — or handheld device available at the site entrance — powered by a Netrasemi chip, with a camera, speaker, and touchscreen. When a visitor points the device at any structure, artifact, or feature, the edge AI recognizes it and provides a rich audio-visual interpretation in the visitor's chosen language — no internet required. The device pre-loads the entire site's content locally, including 3D reconstructions, historical context, and storytelling.

**Key features:**
- Visual recognition of 500+ site features per heritage monument
- 10+ language audio output including Hindi, English, Tamil, Telugu, French, Mandarin, Arabic
- Offline: works without any connectivity at the site
- Accessible: audio-first for visitors with low literacy
- Content updates pushed when device docks at the site office (via local WiFi)
- Kiosk or handheld rental model (₹50–100 per visit)

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Remote heritage sites (Hampi, Chola temples, Ajanta) have no mobile connectivity
- Image recognition for heritage features must be near-instant (<1 second) for a natural visitor experience
- Multilingual NLP inference for contextual narration is computationally intensive
- A cloud-dependent device that fails at the site ruins the tourist experience

---

## Desirability

Tourists want immersive, informative experiences. Site managers want to improve reviews and visitor satisfaction. Tourism departments want to increase revenue per visitor. Conservationists want interpretation that builds appreciation and reduces damage behavior.

**Desirability Score: 8/10**

---

## Feasibility

Visual recognition of heritage features (architectural elements, sculptures, paintings) is feasible with purpose-built datasets. Multilingual TTS (Text-to-Speech) is well-developed and runs on edge. Content creation (scripts, audio, 3D models) is the main effort, not the AI engineering.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

---

## Viability

**Revenue models:**
- Device rental at site: ₹50–100 per visit
- Revenue share with ASI/state tourism department
- White-label for travel app companies (MakeMyTrip, TripAdvisor)
- Premium content licensing: site-specific AR overlays sold to tour operators

**Viability Score: 7/10**

## Possible MVP

A kiosk-based heritage guide for one site (e.g., Hampi) with 50 feature recognition points in 5 languages, deployed for one tourist season. Track: usage rate, tourist satisfaction score, language distribution of users.

**Timeline:** 12 weeks for first site deployment
