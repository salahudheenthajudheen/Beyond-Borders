# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Beyond Borders is a global open innovation community. We pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

**We expect contributors to:**

- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Welcome diverse perspectives, especially from underrepresented communities
- Give and receive feedback generously and gracefully
- Focus on the quality of ideas, not the identity of contributors
- Credit others' work appropriately
- Approach disagreements with curiosity, not defensiveness

**We do not tolerate:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Dismissing contributions based on who someone is, rather than what they've contributed
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of work
- Spam, low-effort submissions, or deliberate noise
- Any form of discrimination

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. A warning from the maintainers
2. Temporary suspension from contributing
3. Permanent removal from the community

To report a violation, contact the maintainers at [The Purple Movement community](#) or open a private issue.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by Beyond Borders — the GitHub repository, pull requests, issues, community forums, and any related events or communications.
