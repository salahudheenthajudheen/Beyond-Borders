# PS-03: Worker Safety Monitoring in Hazardous Manufacturing Environments

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Smart Manufacturing AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Vision Chip + Multi-Camera Safety System

---

## Problem Statement

India records 48,000+ industrial accident deaths and 3.7 million injuries annually, making it one of the most dangerous manufacturing environments in the world. Most accidents are preventable: workers entering hazardous zones without PPE, operating near machinery without safety guards, working in areas with toxic gas buildup, or becoming incapacitated (heat stroke, electrocution) without an immediate alert.

CCTV cameras exist in most factories — but no one is watching them continuously. Safety officers cannot monitor 50 cameras simultaneously. Compliance is checked during audits, not in real time.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Factory safety officers (EHS teams)
- Workers in foundries, chemical plants, construction sites, and heavy industry
- HR and operations heads managing labor compliance
- Factory insurance providers
- Regulators (DGFASLI — Directorate General of Factory Advice Service)

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Each industrial fatality costs a factory ₹5–20 lakh in compensation, legal fees, and production disruption
- Labor unrest triggered by workplace accidents halts production for days to weeks
- International buyers (apparel, auto) now require safety audit certifications — non-compliance loses contracts
- Amendments to India's Factories Act increase personal liability for safety officers

---

## Proposed Solution

An edge AI safety monitoring system — Netrasemi chip + existing CCTV camera integration — that continuously analyzes factory video for real-time safety violations. The system detects: (1) PPE non-compliance (no helmet, no vest, no gloves near hazard zones), (2) unauthorized zone entry, (3) worker falls or incapacitation, (4) dangerous proximity to moving machinery. Alerts are sent instantly to the safety officer's phone and displayed on a floor monitor — not as a log to review later, but as an actionable real-time intervention.

**Key features:**
- Integrates with existing CCTV — no new cameras required in most cases
- Detects 12 PPE types and 8 safety violation categories
- Zone-based access control: alert when unauthorized workers enter hazard zones
- Worker incapacitation detection (fall, collapse, no movement for 30+ seconds)
- Privacy-preserving mode: faces blurred in stored footage; only violation events stored
- Real-time dashboard + monthly compliance report for auditors

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Continuous video from 20–50 cameras cannot be streamed to cloud in real time
- Alert latency must be under 5 seconds — cloud processing defeats the real-time safety purpose
- Worker video footage is HR-sensitive — keeping it on-premise is a legal and ethical requirement
- Factory OT networks are typically air-gapped from internet

---

## Desirability

Safety officers want a second pair of eyes they can actually trust. Factory owners want to avoid accidents and the associated costs. Workers want a safer environment (once trust is established). Insurers want evidence of active safety monitoring to reduce premiums. Buyers want factory safety certification data.

**Desirability Score: 9/10**

---

## Feasibility

PPE detection and person detection AI models are mature — multiple open datasets exist (PPE detection datasets, SafetyDet). Real-time video analytics on Netrasemi chips is feasible. The integration with existing CCTV infrastructure (various camera brands, resolutions, angles) is the primary engineering complexity.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|

| Alert fatigue for safety officers | Medium | Smart alert prioritization; aggregate daily reports |
