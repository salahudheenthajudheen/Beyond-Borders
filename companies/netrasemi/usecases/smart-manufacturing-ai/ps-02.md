# PS-02: Predictive Maintenance for Industrial Machinery in SME Factories

> **Company:** Netrasemi
> **Vertical:** Smart Manufacturing AI
> **Technology:** Edge AI Chip + Vibration + Acoustic + Thermal Sensors

---

## Problem Statement

Unexpected machine breakdowns cost India's manufacturing sector an estimated $10–15B annually in lost production, emergency repair costs, and scrapped work-in-progress. In SME factories, where machines run 16–20 hours a day and maintenance is reactive (fix it when it breaks), a single critical failure can halt production for 2–5 days. Predictive maintenance — detecting failure signatures before breakdown — requires continuous multi-sensor monitoring and AI analysis. The industrial IoT platforms that provide this (GE Predix, Siemens MindSphere) are designed for large enterprises and cost $50,000–$200,000 per machine per year.

SME manufacturers — which make up 95% of India's manufacturing base — cannot access this capability.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- CNC machine operators and maintenance teams in SME factories
- Factory floor managers tracking OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)
- Industrial equipment distributors (profit from service contracts)
- Manufacturing finance providers who take equipment as collateral

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Unplanned downtime costs 5–20% of total manufacturing capacity annually
- Spare parts are often unavailable immediately, extending downtime from hours to days
- Machine failures in food, pharma, or chemical plants can be safety events, not just production events
- Insurance premiums for manufacturing equipment rise significantly with poor maintenance records

---

## Proposed Solution

A clip-on edge AI sensor module — Netrasemi chip + 3-axis vibration sensor + acoustic emission sensor + thermal sensor — that attaches to any industrial machine (motor, gearbox, spindle, pump, compressor) without requiring machine modification. The module runs anomaly detection and failure pattern recognition locally, learning the machine's normal signature during a 2-week baseline period and then alerting maintenance teams when deviation patterns indicate impending failure. The device transmits only alert events (not raw sensor streams) via LoRa or WiFi.

**Key features:**
- Non-invasive: no machine modification, no downtime to install
- Self-learns machine baseline without labeling historical failure data
- Detects: bearing failure, imbalance, misalignment, lubrication loss, motor winding degradation
- Provides estimated time-to-failure and recommended action
- Cloud-optional: works fully offline; dashboard sync when available
- Battery-powered: 6–12 months per battery cycle

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- Vibration and acoustic data generate 1–10MB per second per sensor — cloud transmission is economically infeasible
- Factory OT networks are air-gapped; cloud connectivity is not available at the machine level
- Real-time anomaly detection requires millisecond-level response to transient failure signals
- Proprietary machine behavior data must stay within the factory

## Feasibility

Vibration-based bearing fault detection is among the most mature predictive maintenance techniques. Self-supervised anomaly detection (Autoencoder, Isolation Forest on edge) is deployable on Netrasemi chips. Hardware assembly (sensor + chip + battery + enclosure) is straightforward. Key challenge: making self-learning robust across the enormous variety of machine types and operating conditions in SME factories.

**Feasibility Score: 8/10**

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| High false alarm rate (alert fatigue) | High | Tiered alerts (warning vs. critical); tune sensitivity per machine type |
| Self-learning struggles with variable operating conditions | Medium | Incorporate operating context (load, speed) into baseline model |
| SME willingness to pay for preventive tools | Medium | ROI calculator; pilot with guaranteed outcomes |
| Sensor placement sensitivity (wrong location = wrong signal) | Medium | Guided installation app; standard placement templates |
